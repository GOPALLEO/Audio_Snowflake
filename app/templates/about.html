How It Works

Each individual line drawing of a single snowflake is a hypotrochoid (pronounced hai-po-TRO-koid).

Hypotrochoids are mathematical roulettes traced by a point P, which is located in a small circle that rolls around the interior of a larger circle. If you played with Spirograph as a child, you may remember this premise.

The shape of the hypotrochoid is determined by the radius of circles A and B as well as by the distance (h) between point P and the center of circle B. If you remember Spirograph, the children's toy, that uses the same geometry.
